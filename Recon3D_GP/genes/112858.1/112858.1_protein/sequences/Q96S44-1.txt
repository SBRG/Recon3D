CC   -!- FUNCTION: Component of the EKC/KEOPS complex that is required for
CC       the formation of a threonylcarbamoyl group on adenosine at
CC       position 37 (t(6)A37) in tRNAs that read codons beginning with
CC       adenine. The complex is probably involved in the transfer of the
CC       threonylcarbamoyl moiety of threonylcarbamoyl-AMP (TC-AMP) to the
CC       N6 group of A37. TP53RK has ATPase activity in the context of the
CC       EKC/KEOPS complex and likely plays a supporting role to the
CC       catalytic subunit OSGEP (By similarity). Atypical protein kinase
CC       that phosphorylates 'Ser-15' of p53/TP53 protein and may therefore
CC       participate in its activation. {ECO:0000250,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11546806}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: ATP + a protein = ADP + a phosphoprotein.
CC   -!- SUBUNIT: Component of the EKC/KEOPS complex composed of at least
CC       GON7, TP53RK, TPRKB, OSGEP and LAGE3; the whole complex dimerizes.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:12659830, ECO:0000269|PubMed:22912744,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:27903914}.
CC   -!- INTERACTION:
CC       Q13137:CALCOCO2; NbExp=4; IntAct=EBI-739588, EBI-739580;
CC       Q5JR59:MTUS2; NbExp=3; IntAct=EBI-739588, EBI-742948;
CC       Q9Y3C4:TPRKB; NbExp=3; IntAct=EBI-739588, EBI-750123;
CC   -!- SUBCELLULAR LOCATION: Nucleus {ECO:0000269|PubMed:22912744,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:27903914}.
CC   -!- TISSUE SPECIFICITY: Highly expressed in testis. Weakly expressed
CC       in heart kidney and spleen.
CC   -!- MISCELLANEOUS: This protein is considered an atypical
CC       serine/threonine kinase, because it lacks the conventional
CC       structural elements necessary for the substrate recognition as
CC       well as a lysine residue that in all other serine/threonine
CC       kinases participates in the catalytic event. TP53RK has protein
CC       kinase activity in vitro, but in the context of the EKC/KEOPS
CC       complex, the catalytic subunit OSGEP switches the activity of
CC       TP53RK from kinase into ATPase (By similarity). {ECO:0000250}.
CC   -!- SIMILARITY: Belongs to the protein kinase superfamily. BUD32
CC       family. {ECO:0000305}.
