CC   -!- FUNCTION: Glycerol-3-phosphate phosphatase hydrolyzing glycerol-3-
CC       phosphate into glycerol. Thereby, regulates the cellular levels of
CC       glycerol-3-phosphate a metabolic intermediate of glucose, lipid
CC       and energy metabolism. Was also shown to have a 2-phosphoglycolate
CC       phosphatase activity and a tyrosine-protein phosphatase activity.
CC       However, their physiological relevance is unclear
CC       (PubMed:26755581). In vitro, has also a phosphatase activity
CC       toward ADP, ATP, GDP and GTP (By similarity).
CC       {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8CHP8, ECO:0000269|PubMed:26755581}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Protein tyrosine phosphate + H(2)O = protein
CC       tyrosine + phosphate. {ECO:0000250|UniProtKB:Q8CHP8}.
CC   -!- CATALYTIC ACTIVITY: Glycerol 1-phosphate + H(2)O = glycerol +
CC       phosphate. {ECO:0000269|PubMed:26755581}.
