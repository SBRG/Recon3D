CC   -!- FUNCTION: DNA polymerase specifically involved in the DNA repair
CC       by translesion synthesis (TLS) (PubMed:10385124, PubMed:11743006,
CC       PubMed:24449906). Due to low processivity on both damaged and
CC       normal DNA, cooperates with the heterotetrameric (REV3L, REV7,
CC       POLD2 and POLD3) POLZ complex for complete bypass of DNA lesions.
CC       Inserts one or 2 nucleotide(s) opposite the lesion, the primer is
CC       further extended by the tetrameric POLZ complex. In the case of
CC       1,2-intrastrand d(GpG)-cisplatin cross-link, inserts dCTP opposite
CC       the 3' guanine (PubMed:24449906). Particularly important for the
CC       repair of UV-induced pyrimidine dimers (PubMed:10385124,
CC       PubMed:11743006). Although inserts the correct base, may cause
CC       base transitions and transversions depending upon the context. May
CC       play a role in hypermutation at immunoglobulin genes
CC       (PubMed:11376341, PubMed:14734526). Forms a Schiff base with 5'-
CC       deoxyribose phosphate at abasic sites, but does not have any lyase
CC       activity, preventing the release of the 5'-deoxyribose phosphate
CC       (5'-dRP) residue. This covalent trapping of the enzyme by the 5'-
CC       dRP residue inhibits its DNA synthetic activity during base
CC       excision repair, thereby avoiding high incidence of mutagenesis
CC       (PubMed:14630940). Targets POLI to replication foci
CC       (PubMed:12606586). {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10385124,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:11376341, ECO:0000269|PubMed:11743006,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:12606586, ECO:0000269|PubMed:14630940,
CC       ECO:0000269|PubMed:14734526, ECO:0000269|PubMed:24449906}.

CC   -!- DISEASE: Xeroderma pigmentosum variant type (XPV) [MIM:278750]: An
CC       autosomal recessive pigmentary skin disorder characterized by
CC       solar hypersensitivity of the skin, high predisposition for
CC       developing cancers on areas exposed to sunlight and, in some
CC       cases, neurological abnormalities. XPV shows normal nucleotide
CC       excision repair, but an exaggerated delay in recovery of
CC       replicative DNA synthesis. Most patients with the variant type of
CC       xeroderma pigmentosum do not develop clinical symptoms and skin
CC       neoplasias until a later age. Clinical manifestations are limited
CC       to photo-induced deterioration of the skin and eyes.
CC       {ECO:0000269|PubMed:10385124, ECO:0000269|PubMed:10398605,
